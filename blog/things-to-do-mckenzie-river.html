Things to Do

20 Best Things to Do on
the McKenzie River, Oregon

By Vida View Resort June 2026 10 min read

The stretch of Oregon between Springfield and McKenzie Bridge is one of the state's great under-the-radar destinations — a corridor of waterfalls, hot springs, old-growth trails, fishing holes, and covered bridges, all strung along one extraordinary river. If you're planning a stay near Vida, here are the best things to do on and around the McKenzie River, organized so you can build the trip you actually want.

On the Water

1. Raft or float the McKenzie

The signature experience. The river offers everything from gentle scenic floats to lively Class II–III whitewater. First-timer? Read our McKenzie River rafting guide. Guests at Vida View get a complimentary guided float included with every three-night stay.

2. Stand-up paddleboard the calm pools

The slower sections near Leaburg Lake and the resort's frontage are ideal for paddleboarding. Vida View provides paddleboards and life jackets at no charge.

3. Float the river on an inner tube

On a hot afternoon, nothing beats a lazy tube float on the gentle water. Bring sandals and sunscreen — or use the resort's complimentary tubes.

4. Fly fishing for trout

The McKenzie is legendary among fly anglers — it lends its name to the McKenzie River drift boat. Native redside rainbow trout and stocked fish make it one of Oregon's premier fisheries. Hire a local guide for a drift-boat day or wade in from a quiet bank.

Waterfalls & Hot Springs

5. Sahalie and Koosah Falls

Two thundering waterfalls within a short, easy loop trail upriver near the headwaters. Among the most photographed spots on the entire McKenzie — and unmissable in spring runoff.

6. Tamolitch Blue Pool

A surreal, glowing turquoise pool where the river re-emerges from an underground lava tube. The moderate hike along the McKenzie River Trail is one of Oregon's most iconic walks.

7. Belknap Hot Springs

Riverside mineral hot springs and gardens upriver near McKenzie Bridge — a classic soak with a view of the water.

8. Cougar Hot Springs (Terwilliger)

A series of natural rock pools in the forest off Aufderheide Drive — rustic, beloved, and worth the short walk in.

Hiking & Biking

9. The McKenzie River National Recreation Trail

A 26-mile trail tracing the river through old-growth forest, past waterfalls and lava flows. Hike a section or ride it — it's a renowned mountain-biking route.

10. Clear Lake Loop

A spring-fed alpine lake so clear you can see a submerged 3,000-year-old forest beneath the surface. The easy 5-mile loop circles old-growth and lava fields — bring a camera for the still morning reflections.

11. Delta Old-Growth Grove Nature Trail

An easy, flat loop through towering ancient Douglas firs and cedars — beautiful for all ages.

12. Proxy Falls

A short loop to a postcard-perfect 200-foot waterfall draping over moss, near the Old McKenzie Highway (seasonal).

Local Culture & Sights

13. Goodpasture Covered Bridge

One of Oregon's most photographed covered bridges, spanning the McKenzie right near Vida. A two-minute photo stop with serious charm.

14. Leaburg Dam & Fish Hatchery

Watch trout at the Leaburg hatchery and stroll the grounds — an easy, family-friendly stop.

15. Drive the Old McKenzie Pass (OR-242)

A seasonal scenic byway over otherworldly lava fields to the Dee Wright Observatory, with Cascade-peak panoramas. Open summer through early fall.

16. Wine and dine along the corridor

Stop at local cafés, the Vida Café, riverside pubs, and farm stands. Eugene's restaurant and brewery scene is a short drive west.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the McKenzie River known for?

World-class fly fishing, clear turquoise water, whitewater rafting, waterfalls like Sahalie and Koosah, the Blue Pool, and old-growth hiking and biking trails — all within easy reach of Eugene.

How far is the McKenzie River from Eugene?

Vida is about a 40-minute drive (roughly 30 miles) east of Eugene and Springfield on OR-126. Eugene Airport (EUG) is about an hour away.

What's the best home base on the McKenzie River?

Vida sits central to the corridor's top attractions. Vida View Resort offers two private riverfront properties with rafting, paddleboards, a pool, sauna, and hot tubs included or on site.

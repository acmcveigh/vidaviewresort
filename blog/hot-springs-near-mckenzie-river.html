Things to Do

Hot Springs Near the McKenzie River
Belknap, Terwilliger & More

By Tom McVeigh · Owner & Host July 2026 7 min read

The McKenzie River corridor sits on the flank of the Cascades, and the same volcanic geology that gave the river its lava fields and its improbably blue water also heats groundwater into some of Oregon's best-loved hot springs. Within an hour of Vida you can soak in manicured riverside mineral pools, wild forest terraces under old-growth cedars, or a hidden pool right at the river's edge.

Here's the local rundown — what each spring is actually like, how to time your visit, and how to build a soak-centered river weekend around them.

Belknap Hot Springs — The Easy, Beautiful One

About 20–25 minutes east of Vida on Highway 126, near McKenzie Bridge, Belknap Hot Springs is the corridor's developed option: hot mineral water piped from springs across the river into swimming pools on the McKenzie's bank, surrounded by a lodge and famously elaborate gardens (locals just call it "the secret garden").

  • Vibe: groomed, family-friendly, easy. This is soaking without logistics.
  • Access: day passes are sold for the pools; availability varies by season, so check ahead before making the drive.
  • Best moment: a cool morning when the steam rises off the pool and the river fog hangs in the firs.
  • Bonus: the gardens and riverside paths are worth an hour on their own.

Terwilliger (Cougar) Hot Springs — The Wild One

Roughly 45 minutes from Vida — east on 126, then south past Blue River toward Cougar Reservoir on the Aufderheide Scenic Byway — Terwilliger Hot Springs is the one that ends up on postcards: a staircase of natural rock pools descending a forested ravine, each pool slightly cooler than the one above, all of it under a cathedral of old-growth.

  • Vibe: rustic and reverent. A quarter-mile forest trail from the road; no buildings, just pools.
  • Access: day-use fee, first-come first-served. Summer weekends fill up — go early, and weekdays are far quieter.
  • Know before you go: Terwilliger has a long-standing clothing-optional tradition. It's respectful in practice, but it surprises unprepared visitors.
  • Check conditions: the springs sit in the Willamette National Forest; confirm current access and fire-season status before driving out.

Bigelow (Deer Creek) Hot Springs — The Hidden One

Small, primitive, and right at the river's edge off the upper highway, Bigelow Hot Springs is a single fern-draped pool in a rock grotto, seating a handful of people. It's lukewarm compared to the others, tricky to find, and beloved precisely because of both. If you're already up the corridor for Blue Pool, it makes a fine detour for the adventurous.

Soaking Tips & Etiquette

  • Hydrate — hot mineral water plus a hike dehydrates faster than you expect.
  • Pack out everything, and skip glass entirely at the natural springs.
  • Keep voices low at Terwilliger and Bigelow — quiet is part of what everyone drove out for.
  • Go early or midweek for any of them in summer.
  • Winter soaks are the secret — steam, snow on the firs, empty pools. Check road conditions first.

Where to Stay: The Soak Circuit

The hot springs bracket the corridor, which makes a riverside base between them the right move. Vida View Resort in Vida sits about 20 minutes from Belknap and 45 from Terwilliger — and it extends the soaking after the drive home. The Bed & Breakfast has the corridor's only heated pool and Finnish sauna, and The River Front House puts a hot tub steps from the McKenzie, on 750 feet of private frontage.

The classic guest itinerary: Terwilliger in the morning, lunch in McKenzie Bridge, Belknap's gardens in the afternoon, then back to Vida for a sauna, a cold plunge in the river off your own bank, and the hot tub under the stars. Frequently Asked Questions

What hot springs are near the McKenzie River?

Belknap Hot Springs (developed riverside pools near McKenzie Bridge), Terwilliger/Cougar Hot Springs (natural terraced pools near Cougar Reservoir), and the small primitive Bigelow pool at the river's edge.

Do I need a reservation?

Belknap sells day passes — check ahead seasonally. Terwilliger is a first-come day-use site with a fee; arrive early on weekends.

Is Terwilliger clothing optional?

Yes, by long tradition. Know before you go.

Where should I stay?

Between the springs, on the river: Vida View Resort — with its own sauna, heated pool, and riverside hot tubs for the nights in between.
